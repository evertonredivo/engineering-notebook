# Engineering Notebook Methodology

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

---

## Purpose

This document defines the standard methodology adopted by the Engineering Notebook for conducting technical investigations.

The objective is to ensure that every laboratory is reproducible, traceable, and technically verifiable.

---

## Research Workflow

Every investigation shall follow the sequence below.

1. Observation
2. Question
3. Background Research
4. Hypothesis
5. Experimental Design
6. Experiment Execution
7. Evidence Collection
8. Technical Analysis
9. Conclusions
10. Documentation

---

## Laboratory Structure

Each laboratory shall contain:

- Objective
- Scope
- Background
- References
- Environment
- Methodology
- Experiments
- Results
- Discussion
- Conclusions

---

## Experiment Structure

Each experiment shall contain:

- Identifier
- Objective
- Assumptions
- Environment
- Tools
- Procedure
- Evidence
- Analysis
- Conclusion

---

## Evidence Collection

Evidence may include:

- command outputs
- packet captures
- screenshots
- photographs
- diagrams
- configuration files
- logs
- measurements

All evidence shall be preserved.

---

## References

Whenever possible, references shall prioritize:

1. RFCs
2. IEEE Standards
3. Official Documentation
4. Source Code
5. Scientific Literature

---

## Version Control

Every relevant modification shall be tracked using Git.

Commit messages should clearly describe the purpose of each change.

---

## Revision

Technical documents shall be periodically reviewed and updated whenever new evidence becomes available.
